Week 3 (Pre-work)

For me the easiest way to wrap my head around each of these, is by thinking of the tech stack as a house.

The HTML is the frame. It's what holds everything together but the information it contains doesn't look very pretty.

The CSS is the coat of paint and the aesthetic section of the stack. It takes the information within the HTML and styles it in such a way as to increase the user experience.

Finally, Javascript. The plumbing and wiring of the house. It is the moving parts that actually allow a webpage, or app, to function and perform tasks. Otherwise, we would be stuck with static information.
